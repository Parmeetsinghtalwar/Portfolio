## How messages should sound (style guide — read this before drafting)
The previous batch read like AI templates. They had:
- Identical 3-part structure on every message
- Words like "exactly", "is the right call", "rings true", "is what the space needed"
- Buzzword stacks: "agent systems", "RAG stacks", "infrastructure layer"
- Same close: "Connecting to follow your work."

A good message instead:
- Sounds like a smart curious student actually typed it on their phone
- References a specific moment, problem, or observation (not a generic compliment)
- Has slight irregularity in structure — vary openings, vary closes
- Sometimes admits a small struggle ("hit a wall last week", "gave up by hour three", "kept duct-taping OAuth flow")
- Mentions GetZoned naturally when relevant (the person should see I'm a builder/founder, not pitching them)
- Avoids polish — contractions are fine, slight informality is fine
- The close is varied: "Connecting.", "Sending a connect.", "Wanted you in the network.", "Following from here."

Example of the SHIFT:
- BAD (AI-feeling): "Hi Karan, Composio is exactly the layer I keep reaching for when wiring agent systems. The auth-managed action surface across 1000+ apps removes a real pain. Connecting to follow your work."
- GOOD (human): "Hi Karan, hit a wall last week trying to write my own OAuth glue for an agent doing email + calendar. Found Composio mid-frustration. Running my own event app GetZoned and tinkering with agents. Connecting."
